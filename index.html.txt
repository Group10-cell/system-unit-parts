        <section id="cpu">
            <h2>Central Processing Unit (CPU)</h2>
            <p>The CPU is the brain of the computer where most calculations take place. It performs instructions defined by software.</p>
        </section>
        <section id="motherboard">
            <h2>Motherboard</h2>
            <p>The motherboard is the main circuit board that holds the CPU, RAM, and other essential components. It allows these parts to communicate with each other.</p>
        </section>
        <section id="ram">
            <h2>Random Access Memory (RAM)</h2>
            <p>RAM is the computer's short-term memory, which stores data that is actively being used or processed.</p>
        </section>
        <section id="hard-drive">
            <h2>Hard Drive</h2>
            <p>The hard drive is used for long-term storage of data. It holds the operating system, software, and files.</p>
        </section>
        <section id="power-supply">
            <h2>Power Supply Unit (PSU)</h2>
            <p>The PSU provides power to the computer by converting electricity from the outlet into a form that the computer components can use.</p>
        </section>
        <section id="gpu">
            <h2>Graphics Processing Unit (GPU)</h2>
            <p>The GPU handles rendering of images and videos. It is crucial for tasks that require high graphical performance, such as gaming and video editing.</p>
        </section>
